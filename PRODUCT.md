# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are the hackathon builder and reviewers evaluating whether the
Track 2 Docker captioning agent is reliable, compliant, and competitive. They
need to inspect real input clips, generated captions, provider routing,
validation evidence, and remaining submission risks quickly.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to win AMD Developer Hackathon ACT II Track 2 by producing
accurate, style-matched English captions for hidden video clips while avoiding
elimination gates: malformed JSON, missing styles, timeout, wrong Docker
architecture, inaccessible image, or leaked credentials. Success means a
repeatable Docker run, strong real-inference quality, visible proof, and a clear
path to final Fireworks judging and public image submission.

## Brand Personality

Expert, competitive, exacting. The interface and copy should feel like mission
control for a serious contest entry: calm under pressure, transparent about
proof, and ruthless about quality gaps.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS marketing, decorative AI-purple gradients, vague "powered by
AI" claims, flashy visuals that hide evidence, brittle demo-only behavior,
overconfident claims without fresh verification, and humor that feels mean,
cringe, or disconnected from the visible video.

## Design Principles

1. Leaderboard first: every improvement must protect or increase accuracy,
   style-match, runtime reliability, or submission compliance.
2. Evidence over vibes: show videos, JSON, provider path, validation output, and
   remaining blockers plainly.
3. Understand once, style deliberately: separate factual video understanding
   from tone generation so captions stay coherent across all four styles.
4. Fail useful: provider limits, missing keys, timeouts, or malformed model
   output must degrade into valid, non-empty, style-safe captions.
5. Taste is a scoring feature: humor should be grounded, specific, safe, and
   varied rather than generic or mean.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for the demo UI. Keep motion restrained and optional,
preserve readable text sizes, support keyboard-accessible controls, avoid
color-only status communication, and prevent jokes about protected traits,
appearance, disability, body, age, or identity.
